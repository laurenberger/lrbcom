Lauren Berger

In August 2016, I joined the Ph.D. program in Educational Neuroscience at Gallaudet University. In this program, I am a resident of Dr. Clifton Langdon's Language and Educational Neuroscience Laboratory.
I graduated with a Bachelor's of Science in Psychology and a minor in Language Science from Rochester Institute of Technology in May 2016. 

My primary research interests explore the plasticity and resilience of the neural substrates supporting the language network. 
I study populations with variations in language and sensory experiences. Studying a combination of the two will inform us of the multiple components that underlie the language neural network and, in turn, feed into other higher-order cognitive processes.

I am interested in working in industry, be it government or private sector. I am open to working within academia, if the position is university-funded.
